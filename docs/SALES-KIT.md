# Sales Kit

Use this file as your talking guide when showing the Noir Bistro demo to prospects.

## Demo flow

1. Start at the hero and let the client feel the atmosphere before you explain anything.
2. Show how the site balances luxury visuals with a real booking path.
3. Scroll through the story, atmosphere, menu, and private dining sections.
4. End on the reservation form and explain that the final client version would connect to WhatsApp, email, or a booking platform.

## What to say

- "This is the kind of high-end digital presence that helps a premium restaurant feel established before a guest even arrives."
- "The visual layer creates perceived value, while the layout still supports reservations and real business goals."
- "This is not a generic template. It is a reusable luxury concept that can be tailored to your cuisine, interior style, and service model."

## Client-facing benefits

- Strong first impression
- Better perceived brand value
- Clear reservation path
- Flexible enough for events, tasting menus, private dining, and seasonal campaigns

## Offer structure

### Entry offer

- One premium landing page
- Reservation section
- Menu preview
- Atmosphere gallery

### Mid-tier offer

- Everything in the entry offer
- Additional sections for events, private dining, chef story, or testimonials
- Brand-specific copy and imagery

### Premium offer

- Full immersive homepage
- Additional pages or sections
- Booking integration planning
- Ongoing updates and seasonal redesign support

## Pricing posture

- Do not sell this as "just a website"
- Sell it as a premium digital brand experience
- Anchor the price against luxury perception, direct bookings, and differentiation from competitors

## Tools to use during pitching

- Use the local demo itself as the primary sales asset
- Use Veo Flow or image generation tools later for mock campaign visuals if the client wants variants
- Use your AI writing tools to quickly retheme copy after the prospect call
